Traditions & Festivals

Festivals throught the Seasons

From blossoms to fireworks, a year of joy

🌸 Hanami (花見) — Cherry Blossom Viewing

  • Season: Spring (late March–early April, depending on region)
  • Tradition: Families, friends, and coworkers gather under blooming cherry trees to picnic, sing, and enjoy the fleeting beauty of sakura.
  • Symbolism: Sakura blossoms represent the impermanence of life — they bloom beautifully but briefly.
  • Fun Detail: People often reserve spots in parks with picnic mats the night before, especially in Tokyo’s Ueno Park or Kyoto’s Maruyama Park.

🎆 Matsuri (祭り) — Local Festivals

  • Season: Year‑round, but summer is most famous.
  • Tradition: Communities celebrate with parades, portable shrines (mikoshi), food stalls, fireworks, and traditional dances.
  • Examples:
    • Gion Matsuri (Kyoto): One of Japan’s biggest, with giant floats.
    • Nebuta Matsuri (Aomori): Enormous lantern floats shaped like warriors.
  • Fun Detail: Festival food is a highlight — takoyaki (octopus balls), yakisoba (fried noodles), and kakigōri (shaved ice).

🎍 Oshōgatsu (お正月) — New Year

  • Season: January 1–3 (the most important holiday).
  • Tradition: Families visit shrines (hatsumōde), eat special dishes called osechi ryōri, and decorate homes with pine and bamboo (kadomatsu).
  • Symbolism: Renewal, good fortune, and family unity.
  • Fun Detail: Children receive otoshidama — envelopes with money — from parents and relatives.
